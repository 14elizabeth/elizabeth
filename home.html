Malala Yousafzai

“We realize the importance of our voices only when we are silenced.”

Malala Yousafzai
(1997–)


Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani activist for female education and the youngest Nobel Prize laureate. She is known for human rights advocacy, especially the education of women and children in her native Swat Valley in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, northwest Pakistan, where the local Pakistani Taliban had at times banned girls from attending school.

Born:- 12 July 1997 , Mingora, Pakistan
Parent(s): - Tor Pekai Yousafzai (mother); Ziauddin Yousafzai (father)
Citizenship:- Pakistan; Canada (honorary);
Known for:- I am Malala; Malala's Magic Pencil; Right to education; Female education
Education: Lady Margaret Hall (2017–2020), MORE
